The Oregon Song

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

"The Oregon Song" was written in 1919 by Samuel Bringg. It was written as a learning method for his children to help remember the states. There were only 39 songs written in all, though, The Oregon Song becoming the most well-known.[citation needed] The refrain of the song varies slightly from person to person, but was originally written as, "Oregon, Oregon. Let's go explore again, in the Oregon wilderness".
